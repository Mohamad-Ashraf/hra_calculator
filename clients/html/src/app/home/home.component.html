HRA Affordability Tool

This tool is for people who have access to a Health Reimbursement Arrangement (HRA) offeredby an employer to help pay for health insurance for 2020. New this year, if you are offered certaintypes of HRAs, you might not be able to receive a tax credit to help you purchase healthinsurance. This tool will help you determine if you can use a tax credit to help pay for aMARKETPLACE plan or if you should use your HRA to purchase an ACA-compliant plan eitherthrough Marketplace or off Marketplace.

There are several types of HRAs, and only some of them require you to use this tool. If youremployer is offering you an HRA that requires this tool, they are required to give you a clearwritten notice explaining the terms of the HRA. Be sure to have your notice on hand when youare ready to use this tool.

Use this tool if...

You have been offered one of the following HRAs that can be used to help buy a health insurance plan on your own:

  • An "Individual Coverage HRA" - this term will appear on the notice from the employer
  • A "Qualified Small Employer HRA" ("QSEHRA") - this term will appear on the notice from the employer

Skip this tool if...

Your employer has only offered you one or more of the following:

  • Any other kind of HRA, such as Expected Benefit HRA
  • A health savings account(HSA)
  • A health flexible spending arragement(FSA)
  • Traditional employer-sponsored health insurance
  • Other types of health benefits
  • No health benefits
